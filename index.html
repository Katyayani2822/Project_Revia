How AI Detection Works

  • **Multimodal Analysis:** We use the Gemini model (`gemini-2.5-flash`) to simultaneously analyze the image (pixels) and the prompt (text instruction).
  • **Image Quality is Key:** Clear, well-lit photos with minimal clutter result in 95%+ detection accuracy. Avoid blurry or dark images.
  • **Focus on Raw Ingredients:** The AI is trained to identify raw produce, packaged goods, and unprocessed meats. It may struggle with complex, pre-cooked dishes.
  • **Structured Output:** The model is constrained to output a clean, comma-separated list to ensure predictable parsing for the ingredient checklist.

Optimal Imaging Tips

Example of a Good Photo:

"A dozen eggs, three ripe tomatoes, a head of lettuce, and a half-loaf of sandwich bread scattered on a wooden cutting board."

Outcome: High accuracy for recipe or donation scheduling.

Example of a Poor Photo:

"A blurry picture of a partially-eaten casserole dish taken in low kitchen light."

Outcome: Low or no detection, high chance of error.
